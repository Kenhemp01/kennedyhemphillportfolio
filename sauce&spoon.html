Sauce & Spoon

A tablet-based ordering system implementation for a restaurant chain.

Project Description

As part of a Google project simulation, Kennedy led the implementation of a tablet-based ordering and payment system for Sauce & Spoon, a restaurant with two locations. The system was designed to improve ordering efficiency, reduce wait times, and enhance the customer dining experience.

The project involved coordinating between front-of-house staff, kitchen managers, and technology vendors to ensure a smooth transition to the new system. Usability testing and staff training were key elements of the rollout strategy.

With attention to team collaboration and stakeholder communication, the implementation successfully modernized the restaurant's ordering process and provided insights for future tech upgrades.

This project demonstrates strong leadership in cross-functional collaboration and effective use of project management tools in practical business settings.

My Contributions

  • Planned the implementation timeline using project management tools and techniques.
  • Led communication between internal teams and external vendors.
  • Developed and distributed user surveys to assess pilot feedback.
  • Created documentation and training materials for restaurant staff.
  • Ensured alignment with stakeholder goals and business requirements.

Technologies Used

  • Google Workspace (Docs, Sheets, Slides)
  • Survey Tools (e.g. Google Forms)
  • Project Management Principles (from Google PM course)
  • Stakeholder Communication Techniques
